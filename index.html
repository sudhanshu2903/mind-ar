Chaukhandi Stupa
4th – 6th Century CE · Gupta Period
Built during the Gupta period, this stupa marks the very spot where the Buddha met his five ascetic disciples upon arriving at Sarnath after his enlightenment at Bodh Gaya. The octagonal Mughal tower atop the mound was commissioned in the 16th century CE, possibly to honour Emperor Humayun's visit. It remains the first landmark on the ancient pilgrimage circuit.
Ashoka's Pillar
c. 250 BCE · Maurya Period
Erected by Emperor Ashoka to mark the site of the Buddha's First Sermon — the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta. The polished Chunar sandstone pillar originally soared over 15 m high. Its famous Lion Capital, four lions standing back-to-back atop an abacus of dharma-wheels, became modern India's national emblem and is now housed in the Sarnath Museum.
Jain Temple · Monastery V
Sarnath · Multi-faith Sacred Site
The Jain Temple here venerates Shreyansanatha, the 11th Tirthankara, believed to have taken birth at this very site. Monastery V is among several ancient monastic complexes excavated nearby, revealing how Sarnath thrived across centuries as a multi-faith centre of pilgrimage, scholarship, and artistic creation.
