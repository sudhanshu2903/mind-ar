Chaukhandi Stupa
✕
Built during the Gupta period (4th–6th century CE), this stupa marks where the Buddha first met his five disciples after attaining enlightenment at Bodh Gaya. The octagonal Mughal tower crowning it was commissioned in the 16th century to honour Emperor Humayun's visit to the site.
Ashoka's Pillar
✕
Erected c. 250 BCE by Emperor Ashoka to mark the spot of the Buddha's First Sermon — the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta. The polished sandstone shaft originally stood over 15 m tall. Its famous lion capital, four lions standing back-to-back, is now India's national emblem and is displayed in the Sarnath Museum nearby.
Jain Temple & Monastery V
✕
The Jain Temple here venerates Shreyansanatha, the 11th Tirthankara, believed to have been born at this very site. Monastery V is among several ancient monastic ruins excavated nearby, showing how Sarnath thrived as a multi-faith centre of pilgrimage and learning for over a millennium.
